# Introduction {: #introduction } 

Launching a standalone Appchain presents certain challenges in terms of interoperability with other blockchains, being the first one to overcome the natural isolation it comes with.

Solo chains operate independently without direct connectivity to other chains, making it difficult to exchange data or assets with external networks. Interoperability requires establishing connections and protocols to enable seamless communication, which can be complex and time-consuming.

Another important aspect to consider is security. Interoperability involves sharing consensus and security mechanisms with other chains, and some mechanisms might lead to an increased attack surface (if, for example, connecting to another chain with weaker security). 

## Interoperability in an Appchain Deployed Through Tanssi {: #interoperability-in-tanssi } 

Deploying through Tanssi Protocol and becoming a part of a wider polkadot ecosystem enables the Appchains to benefit from the native interoperability through XCM (Cross Consensus Message Format) and its associated protocol XCMP, facilitating asset transfer capabilities and information exchange, without compromising the security aspects.

An important aspect of the polkadot architecture is that while it benefits from native interoperability it also benefits from a security model that can help contain the impact and prevent the compromise from spreading from one ContainerChain to another, protecting the integrity and security of the overall Polkadot ecosystem.
